This course introduces students to the challenges in designing and controlling legged robots, focusing on understanding key dynammical primitives whose composition allows more complicated behaviors to emerge from simple constituents.  As a TA for this course in both 2021 and 2020, my primary responsibility was to assist the students with the lab component, in which worked with a series of models simulated in Gazebo to perform PD control on toe position, investigate multiple single-leg hopping controllers (including fore-aft velocity and pitch stabilization), and implement dynamic gaits on a model of a planar quadruped (based on Minitaur).  I helped design some of the projects for quadrupedal gaits, and assisted the students in learning how to use the simulation environment and debugging their code.
