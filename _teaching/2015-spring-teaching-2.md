As a TA for this course in both 2021 and 2020, my primary responsibility was to assist the students with the lab component.  The course covered the foundational principles of legged locomotion, including kinematics, dynamics, control, and an introduction to understanding and controlling the hybrid dynamics of simple hoppers and quadrupeds.  For the lab component, students worked with a series of models simulated in Gazebo to perform PD control on toe position, investigate multiple single-leg hopping controllers (including fore-aft velocity and pitch stabilization), and implement dynamic gaits on a model of a planar quadruped (based on Minitaur).  I helped design some of the projects for quadrupedal gaits, and assisted the students in learning how to use the simulation environment and debugging their code.
