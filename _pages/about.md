I develop agile and robust locomotion behaviors for quadrupedal robots.  My dissertation research has focused on developing compositions of biologically-inspired dynamical models to create reliable controllers for locomotion in 3D space.  This work includes analyzing the dynamics of these models to obtain formal stability guarantees, developing new compositions, and refining these controllers through testing in simulation and on hardware.  In addition to my research on model-based controllers, I have studied optimization-based methods for control, including MPC and reinforcement learning.

I am currently in the final months of my PhD program.  I'm excited to apply what I've learned during my research to a new set of problems in robotics.
